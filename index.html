Research Experience

Exploring Natural Language Processing (NLP) in Wafer Defect Recognition

UESTC, Instructor: Dr. Huan Wang (2020.11-2021.8)

  • Researched the application of a novel NLP method called Transformer in wafer defect recognition for the first time

  • Proposed an effective CNN-based attention module and combined it with vision Transformer to construct a framework called MSF-Trans for defect recognition; obtained an accuracy of around 97.5% on a mixed-type wafer defect dataset, and excelled most other works.

  • Finished a paper “Mixed-Type Wafer Defect Recognition with Multi-Scale Information Fusion Transformer” individually; the paper was accepted by the IEEE Transactions on Semiconductor Manufacturing.

Exploring Convolution Neural Network’s (CNN’s) Dynamicity

UESTC, Instructor: Dr. Huan Wang (2021.10-2022.2)

  • Ameliorated vanilla CNN’s drawbacks by endowing it with dynamicity; proposed two types of dynamicity in different dimensions: trans-channel dynamicity and trans-pixel dynamicity.

  • Proposed a novel dynamic convolution called multi-faceted convolution; implemented it in a popular neural network called ResNet, and reached an accuracy of around 98.5% over a mixed-type wafer defect dataset.

  • Finished a paper named “Mixed-type Wafer Defect Pattern Recognition Framework based on Multi-faceted Convolution” individually; the manuscript is under review by IEEE Transactions on Instrument & Measurement after the first major revision.

Exploring Signal Processing Approach in Deep Learning

UESTC, Instructor: Dr. Huan Wang (2022.3-present)

  • Researched the application of a powerful signal processing approach named wavelet transform in image classification and computer vision.

  • Combined discrete wavelet transform (DWT) with CNN to enhance CNN’s noise-robustness and interpretability; proposed a DWT-Layer to enhance CNN’s feature learning ability over frequency information.

  • Designed a framework named MRWA-Net to implement DWT-Layer; outperformed other state-of-the-art methods under different noise levels upon a dataset of mixed-type defects.

  • Finished a paper named “Wavelet Integrated Attention Network with Multi-Resolution Frequency Learning for Mixed-type Wafer Defect Recognition” individually; submitted to IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems.
